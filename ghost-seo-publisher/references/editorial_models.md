# Editorial Models

Use these outlets as orientation points for tone, breadth, and story selection.

## The Verge

Primary model.

Useful takeaways from Vox Media descriptions of The Verge:

- it was founded to examine how technology changes life and the future for a mainstream audience
- it aims to tell the full story of technology
- it combines news, explanation, reviews, and commentary on why a development matters
- it is broad rather than narrowly service-oriented

Use this as the closest benchmark for Tech With Life.

## Engadget

Useful model for:

- consumer tech news
- reviews
- buying advice
- some coverage across entertainment and gaming

Engadget describes itself as covering technology news and reviews, and the intersection of technology, gaming, and entertainment.

## WIRED

Useful model for:

- technology as a force in business, politics, culture, and science
- broader features and social impact stories
- coverage that connects devices and platforms to larger systems

## Ars Technica

Useful model for:

- news, reviews, and analysis
- strong explainers
- technical depth when needed

Do not copy Ars too literally. Tech With Life should remain more mainstream-reader friendly.

## Gizmodo

Useful model for:

- internet culture
- consumer tech voice
- a lighter, more conversational tone when the subject supports it

## Translation into site strategy

Tech With Life should feel like:

- mostly Verge-like in breadth and editorial ambition
- supported by Engadget-like reviews and buying advice
- occasionally informed by WIRED-style societal framing
- sometimes using Ars-level explanation, but in plainer language
- willing to be lively and current in the way Gizmodo can be

It should not feel like:

- a narrow productivity blog
- a self-help blog with gadgets added on
- an enterprise IT publication
- a hardcore enthusiast forum
