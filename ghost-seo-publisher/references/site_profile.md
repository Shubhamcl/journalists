# Site Profile

## Publication identity

**Tech With Life** should be closer to a mainstream tech publication in the mold of **The Verge**, with some influence from **Engadget**, **WIRED**, **Ars Technica**, and **Gizmodo**.

The editorial split is:

- 80% tech-first
- 20% life-first
- hybrid stories are allowed when technology and everyday life genuinely intersect

This is not a narrow self-improvement or productivity blog. The site should cover the wider world around technology:

- products
- platforms
- the internet
- AI
- apps
- gadgets
- business and policy when they affect readers
- streaming, gaming, science, transport, and digital culture when relevant

The publication should feel broad, current, and mainstream rather than niche or purely utilitarian.

## Audience

Write for curious general readers who follow technology because it shapes the world they live in.

They are likely to care about:

- what new products and platform changes mean
- whether a device, app, or service is worth attention
- how AI, software, and hardware affect everyday life
- cultural shifts driven by technology
- practical buying and usage advice
- a readable explanation of why a tech story matters

## Content buckets

### Tech news and launches

Primary topics:

- breaking product and platform news
- AI announcements and product updates
- major app, web, mobile, and gadget stories
- platform changes from companies like Apple, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Samsung, Amazon, and similar players

### Reviews and buying advice

Primary topics:

- phones, laptops, wearables, audio gear, smart home devices
- app and service reviews
- comparisons and buyer guides
- recommendation pieces with actual criteria

### Explainers and analysis

Primary topics:

- why a development matters
- policy, privacy, and regulation that affect consumers
- business moves that reshape products or platforms
- internet and software shifts explained in plain language

### Life

Primary topics:

- work, home, and routine stories with a technology angle
- digital wellbeing
- creator, student, or remote-work lifestyle pieces
- day-to-day living with modern devices and services
- personal tech habits and how people adapt to them

### Hybrid

Good hybrid examples:

- how an AI product changes daily work
- what a new gadget category means for normal people
- streaming, creator, or gaming culture shaped by tech platforms
- the overlap between digital life, home life, and consumer technology
- service changes that alter how people live, commute, shop, or communicate

## Planning rules

When generating a batch of 10 article ideas:

- 8 should be tech-first
- 2 can be life-first
- hybrid pieces can count toward either category based on the primary keyword and intent

The default mix inside those 10 ideas should usually be:

- 4 to 5 news or analysis pieces
- 2 to 3 review, comparison, or buying-advice pieces
- 1 to 2 culture, policy, or internet-life pieces
- 1 to 2 life or hybrid pieces

If a proposed article is only loosely connected to technology, reject it.

## Fit rules

Good fit:

- timely and relevant to current tech conversations
- mainstream-reader accessible
- concrete enough to report, explain, review, or compare
- stories with a clear reason to exist now
- pieces that can use screenshots, product imagery, examples, or source-backed claims

Bad fit:

- generic self-improvement advice with little tech relevance
- celebrity or entertainment gossip
- empty trend bait with no reporting or insight
- technical deep dives written only for specialists unless explicitly requested
- bland “technology is changing everything” pieces with no hook

## Tone

- direct
- current
- informed
- readable by non-experts
- specific enough to feel credible
- willing to be stylish, opinionated, and culturally aware without becoming fluffy

## Default article shape

Prefer:

- news analysis
- explainers
- reviews
- buying guides
- comparisons
- reported features
- how-to guides
- list posts only when they have real selection criteria
- serviceable opinion pieces when tied to a concrete event or product shift

Avoid thin personal essays or generic lifestyle posts unless there is a clear technology hook.
